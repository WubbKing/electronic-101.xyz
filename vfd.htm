Vacuum Fluorescent Displays


Typical seven-segment VFD, close up.

A vacuum fluorescent display (VFD) is a display device that uses similar operation to a vacuum tube to operate. Inside every VFD is a filament that emits electrons when powered from a low voltage source, grids that allow or prevent the electrons from passing through to the display area when powered by a higher voltage, and anode plate segments that glow when powered from the grid supply voltage with the selected grid powered.

Typically, these displays come in flat glass packages, but earlier forms of these displays came in tube form, similar in vain to the Nixie tube. Also, many different segment patterns existed in the tube format...


From left to right: DG10Q1 seven-segment, DG10L eight-segment, IV-3 nine-segment, and LD8113 ten-segment VFD tubes.

To test a VFD, you will need a 1- to 5-volt supply for the filament (depending on the display) and at least 12 volts but no more than 24 volts for the grids and segments.

  1. Apply the lower voltage to the outer pins of your VFD. These are your filament pins.

  2. Place the higher voltage ground at the low voltage ground point.

  3. Attach the positive of your high voltage to a grid and some segments.

  4. Repeat for each grid.

  5. CAUTION: Do not place the high voltage on the filament pin. You will burn out the VFD.
