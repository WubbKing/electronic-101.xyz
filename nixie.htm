Nixie Tubes

A word of warning before reading on: Nixie tubes require a high voltage (approx. 170V DC) to light! Replicate any Nixie tube circuit at your own risk... mishandling the high voltage line can lead to fatal injuries.

A Nixie tube is a display device that uses a gas, neon in this case, to light numeral-shaped wire forms inside the tube. The gas is ignited at a relatively high voltage, about 170 to 200 volts DC, and a selected numeral glows when the anode grid is referenced to the high voltage positive and the numeral electrode is grounded.


Typical Nixie tube. Model GR110.

Attach a 10K resistor (smaller value for larger tubes) to the cathode side of your supply's diode (after the AC) to limit the current to the tube, then attach that to the anode pin of your tube.

The other side of the AC line connects directly to a cathode. The tube should light.

CAUTION: Do not short the supply.
