Driving an LD8113 VFD Tube

In this tutorial, we will learn how to drive an NEC LD8113 VFD tube with simple 74-series logic.

The key to this tutorial is to have it display numerals 0...9 with a centered 1.

This logic is just like Gottlieb's logic as used on pinball tables from Cleopatra SS (1977) to Ice Fever (1985).

So we have our VFD tube... now what? We can't just run everything off of 5V... the filament will burn out if we do that! And that voltage is also too weak for the grid and anode plates.

What we do is use a buck converter set between 1 and 2V for the filament and a boost converter set about 18V for the grid and segments. This gives a nice, bright display.

Then for the logic, we use a 74LS48 with BCD paralleled to a 74LS04 and a 74LS21. We invert lines B, C, and D with three stages of the 74LS04, leave A noninverted, and feed them into a four-input AND from half the 74LS21. This output gives us both the extra segment for the 1 and an input for another stage of the 74LS04, giving us an inverted output for the blanking input of the 74LS48.

But... where does 18V come in? We use a source driver, such as the UDN6118A or the UDN2981A, to drive the segments at 18V from TTL logic signals.

Follow this schematic and you should get a 0 on startup with all BCD inputs grounded. With a BCD code of 0001 loaded, the 1 should center itself on the extra segment in the display. All other numerals should appear as normal.
